    问题 (IPhO 2025 Q2_C4)

    背景：Cox 的时钟

    1765年，英国钟表匠 James Cox 发明了一种唯一能量来源是大气压波动的时钟。Cox 的时钟使用两个装有水银的容器。大气压的变化导致水银在容器之间移动，两个容器相对移动，这种运动为实际时钟提供能量。

    Part C - Cox 的时钟简化模型

    我们研究一个简化版本，包括：

    • 一个圆柱形底部水槽，装有水银浴
    • 一个双部分气压管，浸入水银浴中（完全排空空气）
    • 水槽和双部分管各由一根缆绳悬挂，两根缆绳通过理想滑轮系统连接到同一个质量为 $M$ 的物体，该物体可在水平表面上滑动
    • 系统中水银的总体积为 $V_{\ell} = 5 \mathrm{L}$

    质量 $M$ 的位置由其质心坐标 $x$ 表示。考虑水平支撑面与质量 $M$ 之间的固体摩擦，滑动时的摩擦力大小记为 $F_{\mathrm{s}}$。两个挡块限制质量 $M$ 的位移为 $-X \leq x \leq X$（$X > 0$）。

    系统参数：

    • 水槽：高度 $H_{\mathrm{c}} = 30 \mathrm{cm}$，截面积 $S_{\mathrm{c}} = 210 \mathrm{cm}^{2}$
    • 管状部分：高度 $H_{\mathrm{t}} = 80 \mathrm{cm}$，截面积 $S_{\mathrm{t}} = 5 \mathrm{cm}^{2}$
    • 球状部分：高度 $H_{\mathrm{b}} = 20 \mathrm{cm}$，截面积 $S_{\mathrm{b}} = 200 \mathrm{cm}^{2}$
    • 大气压振幅 $A = 5 \times 10^{2} \mathrm{Pa}$，周期 $\tau_{1} = 1$ 周

    在实际的 Cox 时钟中，机构提供的能量通过棘轮系统储存并用于提升配重。在这个简化模型中，时钟回收的能量对应于水平表面对质量 $M$ 施加的摩擦力所耗散的能量。记 $W$ 为一个周期 $\tau_{1}$ 内固体摩擦力耗散的能量。在其他条件不变的情况下，可以调整 $F_{\mathrm{s}}$ 和 $X$ 来最大化能量 $W$；我们将最优情况下的各自值记为 $F_{\mathrm{s}}^{\star}$ 和 $X^{\star}$。

    问题 (C.4)：考虑 $S_{\mathrm{b}} \simeq S_{\mathrm{c}}$ 和 $S_{\mathrm{t}} \ll S_{\mathrm{b}}$，确定 (1) $F_{\mathrm{s}}^{\star}$ 和 (2) $X^{\star}$ 关于 $\rho, g, S_{\mathrm{c}}$ 和 $A$ 的表达式。(3) 表达相应的最大能量 $W^{\star}$，(4) 然后在 $A = 5 \times 10^{2} \mathrm{Pa}$ 时计算其以 $\mathrm{mJ}$ 为单位的数值。

    To determine the optimal parameters for maximizing the energy dissipated by solid friction in Cox's timepiece, we analyze the system under the assumption that $S_b \approx S_c$ and $S_t \ll S_b, S_c$. The energy dissipated per period $\tau_1$ is given by:

    $$W = 4 F_s X$$

    This expression arises from the fact that the mass $M$ moves between $-X$ and $X$, covering a total distance of $4X$ per cycle. The constraint on the system comes from the pressure fluctuation $P_1(t)$ with amplitude $A$, which must be sufficient to overcome both the restoring force due to mercury displacement and the friction force.

    (1) Optimal Friction Force $F_s^*$

    The maximum force provided by atmospheric pressure fluctuations is $S_c A$, where $S_c$ is the cross-sectional area of the cistern. This force must balance the sum of the restoring force and the friction force at the stop position $x = X$:

    $$S_c A = 2 S_c \rho g X + F_s$$

    Solving for $F_s$:

    $$F_s = S_c (A - 2 \rho g X)$$

    Substituting into the energy expression $W = 4 F_s X$:

    $$W = 4 S_c (A - 2 \rho g X) X$$

    To maximize $W$, take the derivative with respect to $X$ and set it to zero:

    $$\frac{dW}{dX} = 4 S_c (A - 4 \rho g X) = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad X^* = \frac{A}{4 \rho g}$$

    Substitute back to find $F_s^*$:

    $$F_s^* = S_c \left( A - 2 \rho g \cdot \frac{A}{4 \rho g} \right) = S_c \left( A - \frac{A}{2} \right) = \frac{S_c A}{2}$$

    (2) Optimal Displacement $X^*$

    As derived above:

    $$X^* = \frac{A}{4 \rho g}$$

    (3) Maximum Energy $W^*$

    Using the expressions for $F_s^*$ and $X^*$:

    $$W^* = 4 F_s^* X^* = 4 \cdot \frac{S_c A}{2} \cdot \frac{A}{4 \rho g} = \frac{S_c A^2}{2 \rho g}$$

    (4) Numerical Value of $W^*$

    Given: $S_c = 210 \, \text{cm}^2 = 0.021 \, \text{m}^2$, $A = 500 \, \text{Pa}$, $\rho = 13534 \, \text{kg/m}^3$, $g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$

    $$W^* = \frac{0.021 \times (500)^2}{2 \times 13534 \times 9.8} = \frac{5250}{265266.4} \approx 0.01979 \, \text{J} = 19.79 \, \text{mJ}$$

    Rounded to two significant figures: $W^* \approx 20 \, \text{mJ}$

    Answering this question requires a multi-step analysis of the simplified model of Cox's timepiece. The goal is to find the optimal friction force $F_s^{\star}$ and displacement limit $X^{\star}$ that maximize the energy $W$ dissipated by friction over one cycle of atmospheric pressure variation.

    Step 1: Analyze the forces and the system's behavior

    The core of the mechanism is the interplay between the atmospheric pressure $P_a(t)$, the positions of the cistern and the barometric tube, and the distribution of mercury between them. This results in a net force on the mass $M$.

    The net force on mass $M$ from the cables is $F_M(x, t) = \frac{S_b S_c}{S_b+S_c} P_1(t) + \frac{\rho g S_b S_c}{S_b+S_c} x$

    The system is bistable. The mass $M$ will be pushed towards one of the stops at $x=\pm X$. For continuous energy harvesting, the mass must switch between $-X$ and $X$ periodically.

    The condition for the mass to start moving from a stop requires that the cable force overcomes the static friction $F_s$. Using the definitions of $\lambda$ and $\xi$:

    $$\lambda \simeq \frac{2\rho g X}{A}, \quad \xi \simeq \frac{S_b+S_c}{S_b S_c} \frac{F_s}{A}$$

    The condition for permanent oscillation is $\frac{\lambda}{2} + \xi < 1$.

    Step 2: Formulate and solve the optimization problem

    The energy dissipated by friction over one period $\tau_1$ is:

    $$W = F_s \cdot (4X) = 4 F_s X$$

    To maximize $W$, we operate at the boundary: $\frac{\lambda}{2} + \xi = 1$

    Using $S_b \simeq S_c$, the constraint becomes:

    $$\rho g X + \frac{2}{S_c} F_s = A$$

    We need to maximize $F_s X$ subject to this linear constraint. Expressing $X$ in terms of $F_s$:

    $$X = \frac{1}{\rho g} \left( A - \frac{2}{S_c} F_s \right)$$

    The quantity to maximize is:

    $$W(F_s) = 4 F_s X = \frac{4 F_s}{\rho g} \left( A - \frac{2}{S_c} F_s \right) = \frac{4}{\rho g} \left( A F_s - \frac{2}{S_c} F_s^2 \right)$$

    Taking the derivative and setting it to zero:

    $$\frac{dW}{dF_s} = \frac{4}{\rho g} \left( A - \frac{4}{S_c} F_s \right) = 0$$

    This gives the optimal friction force:

    $$F_s^{\star} = \frac{A S_c}{4}$$

    And the corresponding optimal displacement:

    $$X^{\star} = \frac{A}{2 \rho g}$$

    Step 3: Calculate the maximum energy

    $$W^{\star} = 4 F_s^{\star} X^{\star} = 4 \left( \frac{A S_c}{4} \right) \left( \frac{A}{2 \rho g} \right) = \frac{A^2 S_c}{2 \rho g}$$

    Step 4: Numerical Calculation

    Given: $A = 5 \times 10^2$ Pa = 500 Pa, $S_c = 210$ cm² = $2.10 \times 10^{-2}$ m², $g = 9.8$ m·s⁻², $\rho \approx 13600$ kg·m⁻³

    $$W^{\star} = \frac{(500)^2 (2.10 \times 10^{-2})}{2 (13600) (9.8)} \approx 0.0197 \, \text{J} = 19.7 \, \text{mJ}$$

    $$W = 4 F_s X$$

    The device works (mass shuttles between the two stops) if the atmospheric-pressure drive $A$ can overcome both the friction threshold and the hydrostatic restoring contribution when the mass is at a stop. Using the parameters defined in the statement:

    $$\xi \simeq \frac{2}{S_c}\frac{F_s}{A}, \quad \lambda \simeq \frac{2\rho g X}{A}$$

    The border of the working domain is $\xi + \lambda = 1$. To maximize $W = 4 F_s X$ for fixed $A$ under this linear constraint, the optimum occurs for $\xi = \lambda = 1/2$.

    Hence:

    $F_s^*$ from $\xi^* = 1/2$: $\frac{2}{S_c}\frac{F_s^*}{A} = \frac{1}{2} \Rightarrow F_s^* = \frac{A S_c}{4}$

    $X^*$ from $\lambda^* = 1/2$: $\frac{2\rho g X^*}{A} = \frac{1}{2} \Rightarrow X^* = \frac{A}{4\rho g}$

    Maximum dissipated energy per period:

    $$W^* = 4 F_s^* X^* = 4 \times \frac{A S_c}{4} \times \frac{A}{4\rho g} = \frac{A^2 S_c}{4\rho g}$$
